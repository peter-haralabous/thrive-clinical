## Qualitative Analysis of Patient & Caregiver Interviews

**Project:** Thrive Health Connect Product Feedback Sessions
**Date:** November 23, 2025
**Version:** 1.0
**Total Participants:** 14 (P01-CH, P02-HS, P03-CM, P04-JC, P05-MB, P06-OV, P07-CC, P08-MK, P09-JC, P10-MT, P11-RM, P13-MM, P14-PS, P15-GB)

**Total Subcodes:** 45+

---

## HOW TO USE THIS CODEBOOK

- **Main Codes** are numbered (1.0, 2.0, etc.)
- **Subcodes** are numbered under main codes (1.1, 1.2, etc.)
- Each code includes:
  - **Definition:** What the code means
  - **When to Use:** Inclusion criteria
  - **When NOT to Use:** Exclusion criteria
  - **Example Quotes:** 5+ quotes from different participants

**Format:** [Main Code].[Subcode]

# MAIN CODE 1.0: INFORMATION MANAGEMENT & ORGANIZATION

**Definition:** All aspects of how health information is stored, accessed, tracked, and maintained across formats and systems.

---

## Subcode 1.1: Information Fragmentation

**Definition:** Health data scattered across multiple disconnected systems without unified view.

**When to Use:** When participants describe information being "spread out," stored in multiple locations, or lack of complete picture.

**When NOT to Use:** Do not code here if the issue is about accessing specific records (use 1.3) or provider systems not communicating (use 6.2).

**Critical Sub-patterns:**
- **1.1a: Multi-Provider Silos** - Different providers maintaining separate records
- **1.1b: Geographic Barriers** - Loss of records when moving between provinces/countries
- **1.1c: Format Fragmentation** - Incompatible formats (paper, digital, different systems)

**Example Quotes:**

1. **P07-CC:** "You know, like you said, it's very scattered. There's some stuff I can find in Health Gateway, but it's very generic, you know, like it'll say I went for a hospital procedure, but it won't say that that was a colonoscopy or any of that kind of level of detail."

2. **P05-MB:** "I think for me and this is I think really specific because I moved across provinces is that I don't have anything like I don't think they share any information once you sign up for like a new you know a new health care system in a new province. I don't think any records are shared interproincially."

3. **P08-MK:** "Like the information I mean you have access to hopefully most or all of your medical information. Maybe not. But even if you do, it's often spread out amongst [different places]."

4. **P11-RM:** "And some of that is due to systems not talking to each other. Others are due to access to their own electronic medical records."

5. **P06-OV:** "I need my rheatologist and my hypertension person to be able to see each other because I'm trying to get one of them to reduce the medications I'm on because they feel overdubbed."

6. **P13-MM:** "I feel like the health care professionals don't always talk to each other."

- **1.2b: Duplicate Form Completion** - Filling same intake forms repeatedly

# CODING GUIDELINES

## General Principles:

1. **Multiple Codes:** Segments can receive multiple codes if they address multiple themes. Always identify the primary code and note secondary codes.

2. **Context Matters:** Always consider the full context of statements. A single quote may contain elements of multiple codes.

3. **Boundary Cases:** When uncertain between codes:
   - If about personal experience with scattered info â†’ 1.1 (Fragmentation)
   - If about providers not communicating â†’ 6.2 (Interoperability)
   - If about valuing relationship â†’ 3.1 (Human Connection)
   - If about insufficient time â†’ 3.2 (Time Constraints)
   - If about AI specifically â†’ 4.1 (AI Concerns)
   - If about data accuracy generally â†’ 4.3 (Data Quality)

4. **Excluded Content:** Do not code:
   - Interviewer questions or prompts
   - Clarifications without substantive content
   - Technical difficulties or meeting logistics

5. **Critical Codes:** Codes marked with ðŸš¨ represent patient safety issues and should be flagged for immediate attention.
